<li>I am <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/person/darius-the-great/
">Darius</a>, the great king, king of kings, king of all nations, king of this earth, son of <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/person/hystaspes-2/
">Hystaspes</a>, the <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/dynasty/achaemenids/
">Achaemenid</a>.</li>
<li>And king Darius says: <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/religion/ahuramazda/
">Ahuramazda</a>, the greatest of the gods, created me, made me king, bestowed upon me this great and beautiful kingdom, and its good horses and its men.</li>
<li>And by the grace of Ahuramazda, my father Hystaspes and <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/person/arsames/
">Arsames</a> my grandfather - these both were living when Ahuramazda made me king in this earth.</li>
<li>To Ahuramazda thus was the desire: he chose me as his man in all the earth; he made me king in all the earth.</li>
<li>I have sacrificed to Ahuramazda. Ahuramazda bore me aid. What was by me commanded to do, that he made successful for me. What I did, all by the favor of Ahuramazda I did.</li>
<li>This palace which I built at <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/place/susa/
">Susa</a>, from afar its ornamentation was brought. Downward the earth was dug, until I reached rock in the earth. When the excavation had been made, then rubble was packed down, some 40 cubits in depth, another part 20 cubits in depth. On that rubble the palace was constructed.</li>
<li>And that the earth was dug downward, and that the rubble was packed down, and that the sun-dried brick was molded, the <a href="/articles/place/babylonia/">Babylonian</a> people performed these tasks.</li>
<li>The <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/misc/cedar/
">cedar</a> timber, this was brought from a mountain named <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/place/lebanon-mountains/
">Lebanon</a>. The <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/place/assyria/
">Assyrian</a> people brought it to Babylon; from Babylon the Carians and the <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/people/yauna/
">Yaunâ</a> brought it to Susa. The <i>yakâ</i>-timber was brought from <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/place/gandara/
">Gandara</a> and from <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/place/carmania/
">Carmania</a>.</li>
<li>The gold was brought from <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/place/lydia/
">Lydia</a> and from Bactria, which here was wrought. The precious stone lapis lazuli and carnelian which was wrought here, this was brought from <a href="/articles/people/sogdians/">Sogdia</a>. The turquoise, this was brought from <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/people/chorasmia/
">Chorasmia</a>, which was wrought here.</li>
<li>The silver and the ebony were brought from Egypt. The ornamentation with which the wall was adorned, that from Yaunâ was brought. The ivory which was wrought here, was brought from <a href="/articles/place/nubia/">Nubia</a> and from India and from <a href="https://www.livius.org/articles/place/arachosia/
">Arachosia</a>.</li>
<li>The stone columns which were here wrought, a village named Abirâdu, in Elam - from there were brought. The stone-cutters who wrought the stone, those were Yaunâ and Lydians.</li>
<li>The goldsmiths who wrought the gold, those were Medes and Egyptians. The men who wrought the wood, those were Lydians and Egyptians. The men who wrought the baked brick, those were Babylonians. The men who adorned the wall, those were Medes and Egyptians.</li>
<li>Darius the King says: At Susa a very excellent work was ordered, a very excellent work was brought to completion. May Ahuramazda protect me, my father Hystaspes, and my country.</li>
